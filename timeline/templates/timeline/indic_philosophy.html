Applied Indic Philosophy

Introduction

The age of Earth is approximately 4.54 billion years, while modern humans (Homo sapiens) have existed for about 300,000 years. It's important to note that all modern sciences and technology owe a part of their origin to the Indic philosophical systems.


Phases of Indic Philosophy


1. Vedic Period (1500 BCE - 500 BCE)


This is believed to be one of the oldest philosophical traditions. It consists of:

  • Vedas (4 major ones): Rigveda (oldest, philosophical), Yajurveda, Samaveda, Atharvaveda
  • Upanishads (Philosophical Texts)
  • Puranas (18 major mythological texts)
  • Vedanta / Brahmasutra (Compiled by Ved Vyas)

Vedas and Upanishads

The oldest Hindu texts include the Rigveda, Yajurveda, Samaveda, and Atharvaveda. Major Upanishads include Prashna, Mundaka, Mandukya, Isha, and Kathopanishad.

Puranas

These describe the creation, destruction, genealogies of gods, and ancient sages. Some major Puranas include Garuda, Matsya, Brahmanda, Bhavishya, Shiva, Agni, Vishnu, Markandeya, Skanda, Padma, Narada, Vamana, Brahma, Bhagavata, and Linga Purana.


2. Post-Vedic Period(9 darshans 3+6)


Nastika (Heterodox Schools)

These schools rejected the authority of the Vedas:

  • Buddhism (founded by Gautam Buddha, ~500 BCE)
  • Jainism (founded by Mahavira, ~500 BCE, 24th Tirthankara)
  • Charvaka (Materialism, atheistic and anti-Vedic)

Buddhism (~500 BCE)

Key branches include Theravada (Traditional), Mahayana (Philosophical), and Vajrayana (Tantric Buddhism).

Jainism (~500 BCE)

The key figure is Mahavira (24th Tirthankara). Jain sects include Digambara (Sky-clad, sect without clothing) and Svetambara (White-clad, wear white robes).

Astika (Orthodox Schools)

These schools accepted the Vedas as authoritative:

  • Samkhya: Founded by Kapila Muni
  • Yoga: Systematized by Patanjali (in Yoga Sutra)
  • Nyaya: Logical school founded by Gautama Muni
  • Vaisheshika: Atomistic school, founded by Kanada Rishi
  • Purva Mimamsa: Ritualistic interpretation of the Vedas, founded by Rishi Jaimini
  • Vedanta (Uttara Mimamsa): Philosophical exploration of the Vedas, systematized by Badarayana / Ved Vyas

3. Vedanta Period (800 CE - 1500 CE)

This period saw the development of three main Vedanta philosophies:

Advait Vedant (non-duality)

  • By Adi Shankaracharya
  • Ramana Maharishi
  • Proposed maya
  • Brahma=soul=you

Vaishnav Vedant (duality and qualified non-duality)

    Vishnu bhakti and rejected Shankaracharya vedant

  • Vishista Advaita : by Ramanuja Acharya
  • Suddha Advaita : by Vallabh Acharya
  • Dwaita Advaita : by Nimbark Acharya
  • Achintya Bheda Abheda : by Chaitanya Mahaprabhu

4. Sikhism (15th Century)

Founded by Guru Nanak Dev and continued by 9 later Gurus. The core belief is Ik Onkar (One God, Oneness).

  • Guru Nanak Dev
  • Guru Govind singh
  • Guru Teg bahadur

5. Modern Indian Philosophy(19th-20th Century Renaissance)

This period saw the rise of Neo-Hinduism in response to British colonial rule and Western rationalism. Key figures include:

  • Raja Ram Mohan Roy (Founder of Brahmo Samaj)
  • Debendra Nath Tagore (Brahmo Samaj/father of Nabindranath tagore)
  • Keshav Chandra Sen
  • NarendraNath Dutt/ Swami Vivekananda (Disciple of Ramakrishna Paramahamsa)
  • Dayanand Saraswati (Founder of Arya Samaj)
  • Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan
  • Osho (Contemporary spiritual teacher)
  • J. Krishnamurti (Philosopher and spiritual teacher)

Common Trends in Indian Philosophy

  • Karma Siddhanta (Law of Karma) except in Charvaka
  • Atman (Concept of Self or Soul), similar to Abrahamic religions' soul concepts
  • Reincarnation / Rebirth, except in Charvaka
  • Panch Mahabhuta (Five elements: Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Space)
  • Moksha (Liberation from the cycle of birth and death)
  • Jagat Mithya, Brahman Satya (The world is an illusion, Brahman is the only truth), except for Charvaka
